03 — Voice & Tone

How We Sound

Every word on goldencaretherapy.com speaks to an exhausted, hopeful parent. The voice is a trusted guide — never a salesperson, never clinical, never a stranger.

💙
Warm & Real
Acknowledge the weight of the journey. Speak like a friend who is also an expert — not a brochure.
"Some days feel heavy. We show up on all of them."
"Unlock your child's full potential!"
🧭
Guide, Don't Pitch
Parents are overwhelmed. Give one clear next step. Remove friction — never add pressure or urgency.
"We handle insurance so you can focus on your child."
"Limited spots — act before it's too late!"
🏡
Home is the Hero
In-home delivery is the core differentiator. Familiar environments = faster progress. Say it on every page.
"We come to you. Your child learns where they feel safest."
"Visit our therapy center for treatment."
👨‍👩‍👧
Family as Partner
Parents are trained, included, empowered. ABA is a team effort. Use "we" and "together" throughout.
"We train you in the same strategies. You're on the team."
"Leave it to the professionals."
📋
Plain over Clinical
ABA is science but speak in outcomes. If a parent Googles the word, rewrite the sentence.
"Proven strategies. Real results. One step at a time."
"Antecedent-based DTT protocol interventions."
🌱
Progress, Not Cure
Never imply "fixing" autism. Frame as growth, independence, and meaningful steps forward.
"ABA isn't about changing who your child is."
"Eliminate problem behaviors for good."

07 — Photography

What We Show

Every image should make a parent feel seen, hopeful, and not alone. Photography is the highest-trust signal in ABA marketing.

✓
Use These
  • Warm natural light in home settings — living rooms, kitchens, backyards
  • Therapist and child in genuine interaction, never posed
  • Parent actively participating in or observing a session
  • Candid emotion: focus, curiosity, small wins
  • Racially diverse families across all 5 states
  • Warm color grading matching the navy/teal brand tones
  • Children ages 2–12 predominantly
  • Real clinic interior photos for Oradell, NJ only
✗
Never Use
  • Sterile white clinical rooms, lab coats, hospital settings
  • Obvious stock photos with unnatural smiles
  • Puzzle piece imagery — problematic in autism community
  • Imagery implying "curing" or "fixing" autism
  • Children crying or in visible distress
  • Cold or desaturated color grading
  • Heavily staged studio-lit family portraits
  • Competitor branding visible anywhere in frame

08 — Patient Journey

The 4-Step Intake Process

This is the exact flow shown on the site. Every ad and landing page should map to one of these steps as its CTA or context.

1
Insurance & Benefit Check
We contact your insurance, confirm coverage, explain everything in plain language.
2
Initial BCBA Assessment
A Board Certified Behavior Analyst observes your child and builds a personalized care plan.
3
Therapy Where It Helps Most
In-home, at school, or in the community — wherever your child is most comfortable.
4
Ongoing Support & Adjustment
Regular progress reviews, parent training, and plan updates as your child grows.
