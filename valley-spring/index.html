Section 01

Mission & Core Values

Every brand decision traces back to these foundations. Compassion, structure, and measurable growth define who we are.

"At Valley Spring Academy, our mission is to create a safe, compassionate, and structured environment where every learner can build confidence, develop independence, and enjoy learning."

Vision

To be the most trusted ESA-approved special needs school in Arizona, recognized for structured, individualized, and compassionate education that empowers every student to reach their full potential.

🤝

Compassion

Every student is met with understanding and empathy. Staff, communications, and programs acknowledge each child's unique journey without judgment.

📋

Structure

Clear routines, predictable environments, and individualized education plans create the stability that special needs learners require to thrive.

📈

Growth

The school measures success through academic, social, and emotional progress. Every program is designed to move students forward at their own pace.

🌟

Inclusion

Diversity of ability is celebrated. Every child is supported, valued, and treated as an integral member of the school community.

👨‍👩‍👧

Partnership

Families and therapists are essential collaborators. The school maintains transparent, ongoing communication to ensure consistent progress.

Section 05

Brand Voice & Tone

Every piece of communication should reflect our values and connect authentically with families navigating special needs education.

💛

Empathetic & Supportive

Acknowledge challenges while providing clear, helpful solutions.

📣

Clear & Informative

Avoid jargon. Explain specialized terms. Keep sentences direct and scannable.

🎓

Professional & Trustworthy

Communicate competence and qualifications without being clinical.

🌱

Positive & Encouraging

Lead with what is possible. Celebrate milestones. Frame challenges as opportunities.

🤗

Accessible & Welcoming

Feel like a conversation with a knowledgeable, caring educator.

Context Tone Example
Website Homepage Warm, confident, inviting "Every child can learn, grow, and thrive in a structured, education-first special needs school."
ESA Information Clear, reassuring, step-by-step "We guide families through the ESA process step by step, from eligibility to enrollment."
Social Media Encouraging, celebratory "Another amazing week of growth at VSA! Our students are building confidence every day."
Parent Email Personal, supportive "We'd love to learn more about your child's needs and how we can help them thrive."
Blog Content Informative, empowering "Understanding how special needs education works is the first step toward finding the right fit."
Enrollment Materials Reassuring, guided "Getting started is simple. Here are four steps to ESA-funded education at Valley Spring Academy."

✅ Preferred Language

  • "Students who learn differently"
  • "Diverse learners"
  • "Individualized education"
  • "Structured learning environment"
  • "Education-first approach"

❌ Avoid

  • "Disabled children"
  • "Suffering from..."
  • "Special ed kids"
  • "Normal students" (when comparing)
  • Overly clinical or deficit-focused framing
Section 06

Imagery & Photography

Visual content should reinforce compassion, structure, growth, and inclusion. All imagery should feel authentic, warm, and natural.

☀️

Lighting

Natural, warm lighting evoking Arizona sun. Avoid harsh fluorescent or cold tones.

😊

Emotion

Capture moments of focus, joy, curiosity, and connection. Avoid frustration or isolation.

🌍

Diversity

Represent diversity of race, ethnicity, gender, age, and ability naturally and respectfully.

📚

Students Learning

Students engaged with materials, technology, or educators in small-group settings.

👩‍🏫

Educator Interactions

Guidance, encouragement, patience. Educators at student level, making eye contact.

🌵

Arizona Elements

Subtle desert light, warm tones, open skies as backgrounds to reinforce geographic identity.

Section 08

Do's & Don'ts

Quick reference rules for maintaining brand consistency across all applications.

🔖 Logo Usage
✅ Do
✓Use the logo in its provided configurations (stacked or horizontal)
✓Maintain the required clear space on all sides
✓Use full-color logo on white or light backgrounds
✓Use a white (reversed) version on dark backgrounds
✓Retain the registered trademark symbol ®
✓Scale the logo proportionally
❌ Don't
✗Rearrange, rotate, or skew the logo elements
✗Place the logo on busy backgrounds without contrast
✗Change the logo colors or apply gradients/effects
✗Recreate the logo using different fonts or icons
✗Remove or obscure the ® symbol
✗Stretch, compress, or distort the logo
🎨 Color Usage
✅ Do
✓Use the exact hex values specified in this guide
✓Verify contrast ratios when pairing text and backgrounds
✓Use VSA Navy or VSA Blue for dark backgrounds
✓Use neutrals generously for visual breathing room
❌ Don't
✗Approximate colors or use "close enough" substitutes
✗Place white body text on VSA Orange backgrounds
✗Use pure black (#000000) as a background color
✗Overuse bright colors — the palette should feel balanced
✏️ Typography
✅ Do
✓Use Poppins for all headings and display text
✓Use Open Sans for all body copy and extended text
✓Maintain the recommended type scale and hierarchy
✓Use sufficient line height (1.6 body, 1.2–1.3 headings)
❌ Don't
✗Use Berlin Sans FB outside of the logo artwork
✗Mix in additional typefaces not in this guide
✗Set body text below 14px web or 9pt print
✗Use all-caps for body text or extended passages
